CMA CGM Global Business Services · GCP · 2026
88%+ mapping accuracy 5,000+ records resolved 70% effort reduction
Problem Statement
  • Over 5,000 customer records in the CRM were stored under subsidiary company names instead of their ultimate parent entities, causing fragmented analytics and incorrect reporting.
  • Manual lookup and validation of parent companies was time-consuming, error-prone, and required domain knowledge that varied across analysts.
  • No scalable automated solution existed to resolve ambiguous corporate hierarchies across diverse industries and geographies.
Solution

  • Created an Audit Agent that evaluated confidence scores and automatically routed low-confidence mappings to human reviewers — ensuring quality without sacrificing scale.
  • Containerised the full pipeline with Docker and deployed scalable workloads on GCP for reliable batch processing.
Challenges
  • Corporate naming ambiguity — many subsidiaries share similar names across industries — required multi-signal validation combining search results, vector proximity, and LLM reasoning.
  • Calibrating the confidence threshold for the Audit Agent to correctly split automated vs. manual review cases without over-routing to humans required iterative tuning.
  • Scaling the retrieval and inference pipeline to handle 5,000+ entities efficiently on GCP without exceeding quota or cost boundaries.

Customer Lifecycle Intelligence Engine
Capgemini · Azure · Databricks · 2022–2025
40M customers ₹7.48 Cr incremental revenue ~80% churn recall ROC-AUC ~0.78 6–7 behavioral personas
Problem Statement
  • Starbucks had ~40 million customers with rich transaction and loyalty data, yet marketing campaigns were entirely generic — the same offers sent to every customer regardless of behaviour.
  • This caused two critical issues: discounts given to customers who would have purchased anyway (revenue loss), and silently disengaging customers going undetected with no early-warning mechanism.
  • Customer data was fragmented across POS, CRM, loyalty, and mobile systems — each with different formats, IDs, and inconsistencies — making unified analysis impossible without a major data engineering effort.
  • The goal: move from mass marketing to precision marketing by deeply understanding customer behaviour, segmenting customers, and predicting churn proactively.
Solution
  • Single View of Customer: Used Azure Data Factory to ingest data from all sources into Azure Synapse; resolved ID mismatches, cleaned nulls, removed duplicates, and standardised product hierarchies using SQL and CTEs.
  • RFM Segmentation: Applied Recency-Frequency-Monetary analysis on Azure Databricks to quickly surface champions, loyal customers, and at-risk groups — giving immediate business-interpretable segments.
  • Advanced Behavioural Clustering: Engineered 100+ deep features (visit gaps, 30/60/90-day trends, basket size, discount usage, product preferences) and applied K-Means clustering — selecting k via elbow method, silhouette score, and business interpretability — producing 6–7 distinct customer personas.
  • Churn Prediction Model: Defined churn as no purchase in 60 days. Built a Random Forest classifier with strict feature/label temporal separation (no leakage), class-weight balancing for severe imbalance, threshold tuning, and hyperparameter optimisation — achieving ~80% recall and ROC-AUC ~0.78.
  • Risk-Tiered Actions: Scored all customers into high/medium/low churn risk and mapped to targeted interventions — strong win-back offers, nudges, and loyalty rewards respectively.
  • A/B Testing & Impact: Validated via controlled A/B test; calculated true incremental lift (removing natural return rate) — resulting in ₹7.48 crore incremental revenue at scale.
  • Deployment: Outputs stored in Azure Blob and Synapse, integrated with CRM systems, pipelines automated via Azure Data Factory.
Challenges
  • Customer ID mismatches across POS, CRM, loyalty, and mobile systems were the hardest data quality issue — requiring extensive SQL-based entity resolution, validation logic, and manual debugging before any modelling could begin.
  • The churn dataset was severely imbalanced; handling this required careful class-weighting, threshold tuning, and prioritising recall over accuracy to avoid missing true churners at business cost.
  • Ensuring strict temporal separation between features (past data) and labels (future churn) was critical — any leakage would have inflated model metrics while failing in production.
  • Selecting the right number of behavioural clusters required balancing statistical validity (silhouette scores, elbow method) with genuine business interpretability — clusters that looked good mathematically but made no sense to marketing teams had to be discarded.
  • Isolating true incremental revenue from A/B test results required careful control-group design to strip out customers who would have returned organically, ensuring the reported ₹7.48 crore figure reflected real model impact.
